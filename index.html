Mundial is the brand's primary headline face, but it lives in Adobe Fonts only. The guide itself documents Poppins as the legitimate web fallback on page 23. We default to Poppins so this document and the eventual landing page render cleanly anywhere, with zero licensing friction. DM Sans handles body. Both faces are Google-served, free, and honor the geometric-sans personality the guide locks in.

Podcast-adjacent imagery. Mics, headphones, hosts at the desk. Minimal retouching, neutral colors. A fallback for utility moments only.

Everything belongs inside the circle.

The loop is the brand's structural primitive, not decoration. It frames photography, anchors typography, contains the monogram, and in the production page would thread through the scroll as a single sustained gesture. We treat it as architecture. That is the difference between a brand that has a graphic device and a brand that is one.
